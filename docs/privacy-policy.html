Privacy Policy

Your finance entries stay local.

Finora is designed as a simple local expense tracker without accounts, ads, backend servers, Firebase, or cloud sync.

Effective date: June 23, 2026

Data stored on your device

Finora stores the transactions you enter, including titles, amounts, transaction type, categories, dates, balance totals, and dark mode preference in local app storage on your device.

No accounts or cloud sync

Finora does not require account creation, login, backend servers, Firebase, or cloud synchronization. Your financial tracking data is not uploaded by Finora.

No ads or third-party selling

Finora does not include advertisements and does not sell, rent, or share your personal finance entries with advertisers or data brokers.

Your control

You can remove Finora data by deleting transactions in the app, clearing app data from your device settings, or uninstalling the app. Because Finora stores data locally, uninstalling or clearing app data may permanently remove your entries.

Device responsibility

Anyone with access to your unlocked device may be able to view information in Finora. Use your device passcode, operating system privacy controls, and backup settings responsibly.

Children's privacy

Finora is a general personal finance utility and is not directed toward collecting information from children. The app does not knowingly collect personal data on a server.

Policy updates

If Finora changes how data is stored or handled, this policy should be updated before release so users can review the current terms.
